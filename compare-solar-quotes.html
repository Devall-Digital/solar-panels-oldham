How to Compare Solar Panel Quotes in Greater Manchester

Expert guide to comparing solar panel quotes and finding the best deal for your home in Oldham, Rochdale, Manchester and surrounding areas.

Getting multiple solar panel quotes is essential to ensure you get the best deal. This guide will help you compare quotes effectively and choose the right installer for your solar panel installation in Greater Manchester.

What to Compare in Solar Panel Quotes

1. System Size and Panel Specifications

Compare the proposed system size (measured in kW) and panel specifications:

  • System size - Does it match your energy needs?
  • Panel brand and model - Quality and efficiency ratings
  • Panel efficiency - Higher efficiency means more power per square metre
  • Number of panels - More panels may mean better coverage
  • Warranty period - Most panels come with 25-year warranties

2. Installation Cost

The total installation cost is crucial, but don't just look at the bottom line:

  • Price per kW - Compare £/kW to ensure fair pricing (typically £1,800-£2,500 per kW)
  • What's included - Scaffolding, installation, inverter, monitoring system
  • What's excluded - Any additional costs or hidden fees
  • Payment terms - Deposit required, payment schedule

Tip: Use our free calculator to get a baseline estimate before comparing quotes.

3. Inverter Type and Warranty

The inverter converts solar energy to usable electricity. Compare:

  • Inverter type - String inverter vs micro-inverters vs optimisers
  • Inverter brand - Quality and reliability
  • Warranty period - Typically 5-10 years, some extendable
  • Monitoring system - App or online monitoring included?

4. Installation Timeline

Compare when each installer can complete the installation:

  • Survey date - When will they assess your property?
  • Installation date - How long until installation?
  • Completion timeline - How many days for installation?

Note: Quality installers may have longer wait times - this can be a good sign of demand.

5. Installer Credentials and Experience

Verify each installer's qualifications:

  • MCS Certification - Essential for Smart Export Guarantee eligibility
  • NICEIC or RECC membership - Industry body memberships
  • Insurance - Public liability and professional indemnity
  • Years in business - Experience matters
  • Local experience - Do they know your area?

6. Customer Reviews and References

Check each installer's reputation:

  • Online reviews - Check Google, Trustpilot, Checkatrade
  • Customer references - Ask for local references
  • Case studies - Examples of previous work
  • Complaints record - Check for any issues

7. After-Sales Support

Compare what happens after installation:

  • Warranty support - How do they handle warranty claims?
  • Maintenance - Do they offer maintenance services?
  • Monitoring support - Help with monitoring system setup
  • Response time - How quickly do they respond to issues?

Red Flags to Watch For

Warning signs that should make you cautious:

  • ❌ Prices significantly below market rate (too good to be true)
  • ❌ Pressure to sign immediately
  • ❌ No MCS certification
  • ❌ Poor or no online reviews
  • ❌ Vague or unclear quotes
  • ❌ Requesting large deposits upfront
  • ❌ Can't provide references
  • ❌ No clear warranty documentation

Questions to Ask Each Installer

Prepare these questions before speaking with installers:

  1. Are you MCS certified?
  2. How many solar panel installations have you completed?
  3. Can you provide references from local customers?
  4. What warranty do you offer on installation work?
  5. What happens if there's a problem after installation?
  6. Will you handle all necessary paperwork (DNO notification, etc.)?
  7. How long will the installation take?
  8. What's included in the quoted price?
  9. Are there any additional costs I should be aware of?
  10. What monitoring system do you recommend?

Making Your Decision

After comparing quotes, consider:

  • Value, not just price - The cheapest quote isn't always the best
  • Quality of equipment - Better panels may cost more but last longer
  • Installation quality - Poor installation can reduce system performance
  • Warranty coverage - Comprehensive warranties provide peace of mind
  • Installer reputation - Choose someone you trust
  • Local knowledge - Installers familiar with your area understand local conditions

Typical Solar Panel Costs in Greater Manchester

To help you compare, here are typical price ranges:

  • 3kW system: £4,500 - £6,000
  • 4kW system: £6,000 - £8,000
  • 5kW system: £7,500 - £10,000
  • 6kW system: £9,000 - £12,000
